Why you need to visit Marselisborg Deer Park in Aarhus ASAP

by Katarzyna Laniecka

Whether you live in Aarhus or plan a trip to this beautiful seaside city, you must visit one of its most valuable sites. A place where you can truly get in touch with nature – Marselisborg Dyrehave.

A deer eating an apple
(Credit: Katarzyna Laniecka)

A bit of info

Established: 1932

Size: 22 hectares

Species: Sika Deer, Roe Deer, Wild Boars

Open from sunrise to sunset

Free entrance

Prepare to get amazed

There is no better way to get close to deer than this. Take a walk through the park and discover how incredibly friendly and peaceful they are. Just make sure to bring carrots or apples (broken into pieces!) and you won’t be able to hide from them.

While you’re there, you’ll have a perfect opportunity to teach your kids about animals, take some breathtaking pictures or spend a nice afternoon picnicking with your friends surrounded by those stunning creatures.

But be careful. Even though deer are incredibly cute, they are wild animals – not pets. Always stay alert and treat them with respect.

A deer eating an apple
(Credit: Katarzyna Laniecka)

Marselisborg Deer Park is in fact a small safari park/zoo/preserve. Animals have plenty of space to live, feed, relax. Of course the deer are in captivity and their lifestyle is far from their original one (constant presence of tourists, regular feeding time, fences), but in comparison to animals in traditional ZOOs this is a great alternative.

Location

The park is about 5 kilometers from the city and you can get there with a 31 bus. On your way back you can also see The Infinity Bridge – an amazing place to relax and admire Aarhus from afar.
